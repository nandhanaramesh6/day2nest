Famous Attractions in Mumbai

Gateway of India

A historic monument built in 1924, it marks the arrival of King George V and Queen Mary in India.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historic railway station is a stunning example of Victorian Gothic architecture.

Elephanta Caves

Ancient rock-cut caves on Elephanta Island, featuring sculptures of Hindu gods.

Juhu Beach

A popular beach in Mumbai known for its vibrant atmosphere and delicious street food.

Haji Ali Dargah

A mosque and dargah located on an islet in the Arabian Sea, known for its spiritual significance and unique architecture.

Bandra-Worli Sea Link

A modern engineering marvel, this cable-stayed bridge connects Bandra and Worli.

Colaba Causeway

A bustling street in Mumbai famous for shopping, street food, and colonial-era architecture.

San Jay Gandhi National Park

A lush green park in the heart of Mumbai, home to a variety of wildlife and the famous Kanheri Caves.

Film City

The hub of the Bollywood film industry, offering tours of the studios and sets.
